## Objective
Teach Kalvin identities M and H, establish their relationship, then introduce a canonical expansion that triggers S2 routing and expansion proposals.

## Approach
Introduce identities, countersign, add a new identity, then submit a canonize that creates a dual-misfit kline. The misfit kline partially matches the countersignature kline, routing S2 and producing expansion proposals.

## Lessons

### 1
Introduce the identity M.

```
M
```

### 2
Introduce the identity H.

```
H
```

### 3
Establish a bidirectional relationship between M and H.

```
M == H
```

### 4
Introduce the identity A.

```
A
```

### 5
Submit a canonize that creates a dual-misfit kline {MH: [H, A]}.
This kline should route S2 against {M: [H]} because H matches but A doesn't.

```
MH => H A
```
